The Seven Wonders of the World

Begin Your Journey

Select a wonder to learn more about its history, explore images and videos, and discover its location on the map.

Great Wall of China (China) Stretching thousands of kilometers across northern China, the Great Wall is one of humanity’s greatest engineering achievements, built to protect ancient civilizations and symbolize perseverance.
Petra (Jordan) Carved directly into rose-red cliffs, Petra is an ancient city famous for its stunning rock-cut architecture and advanced water systems, earning it the nickname “The Rose City.”
Colosseum (Italy) Standing at the heart of Rome, the Colosseum is an iconic symbol of the Roman Empire. This massive amphitheater once hosted gladiatorial contests and public spectacles, showcasing Rome’s architectural brilliance and cultural influence.
Machu Picchu (Peru) Perched high in the Andes Mountains, Machu Picchu is a mysterious Incan citadel surrounded by mist and lush greenery. Its precisely crafted stone structures and stunning location reflect the ingenuity of an ancient civilization.
Chichén Itzá (Mexico) A powerful center of the Maya civilization, Chichén Itzá is renowned for its astronomical precision. The iconic El Castillo pyramid aligns with the sun during equinoxes, revealing the Mayans’ advanced knowledge of science and time.
Christ the Redeemer (Brazil) Overlooking Rio de Janeiro from Mount Corcovado, Christ the Redeemer is a global symbol of peace and unity. With arms outstretched, this towering statue offers a welcoming presence and panoramic views of the city below.
Taj Mahal (India) The Taj Mahal is a masterpiece of white marble and timeless beauty. Built as a monument of love, its symmetrical design, intricate details, and serene gardens make it one of the most admired structures in the world.
