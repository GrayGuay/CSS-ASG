Location of the Place

Overview

The Taj Mahal is a magnificent white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India. Built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, it was constructed in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. The Taj Mahal is renowned for its stunning symmetry, intricate carvings, and beautiful gardens. It is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

History

Construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and was completed in 1653, employing thousands of artisans and craftsmen. The complex includes a mosque, guest house, gardens, and reflecting pools, all designed in the traditional Mughal style. Over the centuries, the Taj Mahal has faced threats from pollution and natural aging, leading to ongoing preservation efforts.
