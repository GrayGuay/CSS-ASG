Description

Chichén Itzá is a large pre-Columbian city built by the Maya civilization located in the Yucatán Peninsula of Mexico. It features iconic structures such as El Castillo, the Temple of the Warriors, and the Great Ball Court. The site demonstrates advanced architectural, astronomical, and cultural knowledge of the Maya people.

History

Chichén Itzá was a major economic, political, and religious center during the Terminal Classic period (c. AD 800–1000). The pyramid of El Castillo was used for astronomical and ceremonial purposes. Learn more about its history on Wikipedia .
