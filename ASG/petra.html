Location of the Place

Overview

Petra is an ancient archaeological city located in southern Jordan, famous for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system. Often called the "Rose City" due to the color of the stone, Petra was once a thriving trading hub and the capital of the Nabataean Kingdom. It is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Learn more on Wikipedia.

History

Petra was established as early as the 4th century BC by the Nabataeans, who controlled major trade routes connecting Arabia, Egypt, and the Mediterranean. The city flourished due to its advanced water management system, allowing it to survive in a desert environment. Petra later came under Roman control in 106 AD and gradually declined after earthquakes and changes in trade routes. The city was largely forgotten by the Western world until it was rediscovered in 1812 by Swiss explorer Johann Ludwig Burckhardt.
