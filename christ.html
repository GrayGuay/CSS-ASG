Christ the Redeemer

Exploring the Place

Location of the Place

Overview

Christ the Redeemer (Cristo Redentor) is an iconic statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Standing 30 meters tall atop the Corcovado mountain, it overlooks the city and is recognized worldwide as a symbol of Christianity and Brazilian culture. Visitors can reach the site via train or road, offering spectacular panoramic views. More details on Wikipedia.

Cultural and Religious Significance

Christ the Redeemer is a powerful symbol of Christianity, representing peace, acceptance, and protection. The statue depicts Jesus Christ with open arms, symbolizing welcoming and compassion toward all people.

Beyond its religious meaning, the monument has become a cultural icon of Brazil and Rio de Janeiro, representing unity and national pride. It is often illuminated during important global events and celebrations.

History

The idea of constructing a giant statue atop Corcovado mountain was first proposed in the mid-1850s, but it wasn’t until 1921 that construction began. The statue was completed and inaugurated in 1931, designed by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa and French sculptor Paul Landowski. Made of reinforced concrete and soapstone, it has become one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

Construction Timeline

1850s – Early Proposal

The idea of a Christian monument on Corcovado Mountain was first suggested by Catholic priest Pedro Maria Boss.

1921 – Project Approval

The Catholic Circle of Rio officially approved the project and raised funds for construction.

1922–1931 – Construction

Construction began, with the statue designed by Heitor da Silva Costa and sculpted by Paul Landowski.

1931 – Inauguration

Christ the Redeemer was officially inaugurated and opened to the public.

2000s – Restoration

Major restoration efforts were carried out to preserve the statue from weather damage.

3 Fun Facts

  • The statue weighs over 600 tons and is made of reinforced concrete and soapstone.
  • Christ the Redeemer is struck by lightning several times each year.
  • The statue’s arms stretch 28 meters wide, symbolizing an embrace of the city.
